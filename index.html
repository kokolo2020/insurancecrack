How much life insurance do I need?
A common rule is 10–12× your annual income plus outstanding debts. If you have dependents, add $100K–$200K per child for education and living expenses.
Term vs whole life insurance?
Term life covers you for a fixed period (10–30 years) and is much cheaper. Whole life covers you permanently and builds cash value, but costs 5–10× more. Most financial advisors recommend term life for most people.
What affects my premium?
Age, health, smoking status, coverage amount, and policy term are the main factors. The younger and healthier you are when you buy, the cheaper your premium.

What is the minimum auto insurance required?
Most US states require liability insurance at minimum — covering bodily injury and property damage you cause to others. Minimums vary by state, typically $25K–$50K per person.
How can I lower my auto insurance premium?
Bundle with home insurance, maintain a clean driving record, increase your deductible, take a defensive driving course, and ask about low-mileage discounts.

Which health plan tier is best?
Silver plans are most popular — they balance premiums and out-of-pocket costs. If you're healthy and rarely see doctors, Bronze saves money. If you have regular medical needs, Gold or Platinum saves on out-of-pocket costs.
What is an out-of-pocket maximum?
The maximum you'll pay in a year before insurance covers 100% of costs. In 2024, the ACA limit is $9,450 for individuals and $18,900 for families.

How much home insurance do I need?
Your dwelling coverage should equal the cost to rebuild your home, not its market value. Rebuilding typically costs $100–$200/sq ft depending on location and materials.
Does home insurance cover floods?
Standard home insurance does NOT cover flood damage. You need a separate flood insurance policy, typically through the National Flood Insurance Program (NFIP) or private insurers.
