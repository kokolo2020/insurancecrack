What Is An Insurance Deductible?

A deductible is the amount you pay before your insurance company begins paying a claim.

Example

If your deductible is $1,000 and you have a covered loss of $5,000, you pay the first $1,000 and the insurer pays the remaining $4,000.

Higher vs Lower Deductibles

  • Higher deductible = lower premium
  • Lower deductible = higher premium
